A Artic.le

Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 16, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Artic.le ("the app", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and protects your personal information when you use our mobile application and related services (collectively, "the Service"). Artic.le is a calm news aggregator that lets you follow sources, read articles, and generate AI-assisted summaries.

Artic.le is operated by an independent developer based in Brazil. By using the Service, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

Summary We collect the minimum data needed to run the app: your account information, which sources you follow, and which articles you tag. We never sell your data. You can delete your account and all associated data at any time by following the instructions at our data deletion page.

1. Information we collect

1.1 Information you provide directly

When you create an account, we collect:

  • Email address — for authentication and account recovery.
  • Display name — shown in your profile within the app.
  • Password (if using email signup) — stored as a secure hash; we never see or store your plaintext password.

1.2 Information from social login

If you sign in using Google or Facebook, we receive the following from the provider, subject to your consent on their platform:

  • Your email address
  • Your public display name
  • A unique identifier that links your account to the provider

We do not request or store your contacts, posts, friends list, photos, or any other data from these providers. You can revoke our access at any time from your Google or Facebook account settings.

1.3 Information generated by your use of the Service

  • Sources you follow — the publishers whose articles appear in your feed.
  • Articles you tag (save) — references to articles you've marked for later reading.
  • Reading preferences — your selected text size, theme (light/dark), and default summary lens.
  • AI summary interactions — when you request an AI summary, we send the article URL to our backend to generate the summary. Your chosen lens and the article URL may be logged for caching purposes, but the content of the summary you read is not tied to your identity in logs.

1.4 Technical and diagnostic information

To keep the Service running and diagnose problems, we automatically collect:

  • Device type and operating system version
  • App version
  • Anonymized crash reports and error logs (via Sentry)
  • Anonymous usage analytics (via PostHog) — e.g., which screens are most used, performance metrics. We do not track your individual identity in analytics.
  • IP address, used transiently for authentication and rate-limiting. We do not store persistent IP logs.

2. How we use your information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Authenticate you and keep your account secure.
  • Deliver the articles from sources you follow.
  • Generate AI summaries of articles you request summaries for.
  • Remember your preferences (theme, text size, followed sources, tagged articles).
  • Diagnose crashes and improve the app's stability.
  • Understand anonymous aggregate usage to improve the product.
  • Communicate with you about important account or service changes (we do not send marketing emails without your explicit opt-in).

3. Legal basis for processing (LGPD)

Under Brazil's Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (LGPD — Law No. 13.709/2018), we process your personal data on the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract (Art. 7, V) — to provide the Service you signed up for.
  • Consent (Art. 7, I) — when you opt in to social login or to any future optional features.
  • Legitimate interest (Art. 7, IX) — for security, fraud prevention, and service stability, always balanced against your rights.
  • Legal obligation (Art. 7, II) — when required by Brazilian law or valid legal process.

4. How we share your information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We share data only with the following categories of service providers, who process it on our behalf under contract:

  • Supabase — authentication and database infrastructure. Data is stored in their secured cloud environment.
  • Cloudflare R2 — storage for any media assets.
  • Sentry — error tracking and crash reporting.
  • PostHog — anonymous product analytics.
  • AI model providers — when you request an AI summary, the article URL and lens selection are sent to our LLM provider to generate the summary. The providers' terms prohibit them from training on our API inputs.

We may also disclose information if required to do so by law, to respond to valid legal process, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of users or the public.

5. Data retention

We retain your personal information for as long as your account is active. When you delete your account (see Section 8), we delete your personal data within 30 days, except where we are legally required to retain certain records (in which case we retain only the minimum required).

Anonymous analytics data and aggregated usage statistics may be retained indefinitely since they are not personally identifiable.

6. Data security

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information, including:

  • All data transmitted between the app and our servers is encrypted in transit (HTTPS/TLS).
  • Passwords are stored as salted, one-way hashes — we never see your plaintext password.
  • Database access is restricted to authorized systems using short-lived credentials.
  • Infrastructure is hosted with providers who maintain industry-standard security certifications.

No system is 100% secure. If we become aware of a data breach that affects your personal information, we will notify you and the Brazilian National Data Protection Authority (ANPD) as required by LGPD Art. 48.

7. Your rights

7.1 Rights under LGPD (for users in Brazil)

Under LGPD Art. 18, you have the right to:

  • Confirm whether we process your personal data.
  • Access your personal data.
  • Correct incomplete, inaccurate, or outdated data.
  • Anonymize, block, or delete unnecessary or excessive data.
  • Port your data to another service provider.
  • Delete personal data processed based on your consent.
  • Obtain information about with whom we share your data.
  • Obtain information about the consequences of refusing consent.
  • Revoke consent at any time.

7.2 Rights under GDPR (for users in the European Economic Area)

If you are located in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, you also have the rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, and objection, as defined under GDPR.

7.3 How to exercise your rights

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at diogodow@gmail.com. We will respond within 15 days (LGPD) or 30 days (GDPR). We may ask you to verify your identity before processing certain requests.

8. Account deletion

You can delete your account and all associated personal data at any time. See our dedicated data deletion instructions for the full process.

9. Children's privacy

Artic.le is not intended for children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will take steps to delete it.

10. International data transfers

Some of our service providers are located outside Brazil. When we transfer your data internationally, we do so under the legal bases permitted by LGPD Art. 33, including adequacy decisions, contractual clauses, or your explicit consent.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will notify you through the app or by email before the changes take effect. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page indicates when the policy was most recently revised.
